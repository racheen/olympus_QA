     <a href="../Pontios/Leukothea.html">
      INO
     </a>
     A nurse of the god Dionysus who was transformed into a sea-goddess after she leapt from a cliff to escape her crazed husband. Renamed Leucothea she became a protectress of sailors.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Heroine/Io.html">
      IO
     </a>
     A Naiad-nymph daughter of the river Inachus  loved by Zeus. When Hera interrupted their tryst the god transformed her into a cow, but the goddess was not fooled and demanded her as a gift.  Io's guardian was later slain and so Hera sent a gadfly to torment her, driving the heifer-maiden to wander all the way to Egypt where she gave birth to Epaphos, ancestor of the pharaohs.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Daimon/Ioke.html">
      IOKE
     </a>
     The female personification of onslaught and pursuit in battle.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiIonides.html">
      IONIDES
     </a>
     Four Naiad-nymph daughters of the river Cytherus in southern Greece whose springs were reputed to possess healing properties.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ouranios/HoraEirene.html">
      IRENE
     </a>
     (Eirene) The goddess of peace and the season of spring. She was one of the three Horae (Seasons).
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Pontios/Iris.html">
      IRIS
     </a>
     The golden-winged goddess of the rainbow and messenger of the gods.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheIsmene.html">
      <span class="name">
       ISMENE
      </span>
     </a>
     A Naiad-nymph daughter of the river Asopus who was the wife of the eponymous Argive king Argus.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheIsmenis.html">
      ISMENIS
     </a>
     A Naiad-nymph daughter of the river Ismenos inBoeotia loved by the god Faunus.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Potamos/PotamosIsmenos.html">
      ISMENUS
     </a>
     (Ismenos) A river of Boeotia and its liquid god.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Potamos/PotamosIstros.html">
      ISTER
     </a>
     (Istros) The god of the Scythian river Ister (now the Danube).
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Titan/Ithax.html">
      ITHAX
     </a>
     Messenger of the Titan-gods.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheIynx.html">
      IYNX
     </a>
     The goddess-nymph of the magical love-charm known as the
     <em>
      iynx
     </em>
     --a spinning wheel to which a wryneck bird  was attached.
    </p>
    <hr/>
    <p id="J">
     <a href="../Georgikos/EleusiniosIakkhos.html">
      JACCHUS
     </a>
     (Iakkhos) The torch-bearing god of the procession of initiates of the Eleusinian Mysteries.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Titan/TitanIapetos.html">
      JAPETUS
     </a>
     (Iapetos) One of the six-Titan brothers imprisoned by Zeus in the pit of Tartarus.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Georgikos/Iasion.html">
      JASION
     </a>
     (Iasion) A springtime consort of the goddess Demeter.
    </p>
    <p>
     JASON (Iason) The celebrated leader of the Argonauts in their quest for the fabled Golden Fleece.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheIynx.html">
      JINX
     </a>
     (Iynx) The nymph of the magical love-charm known as the
     <em>
      iynx
     </em>
     .
    </p>
    <hr/>
    <p id="K">
     <a href="../Daimon/Keres.html">
      KERES
     </a>
     Monstrous female spirits of violent death which haunted the battlefield ripping souls free from the bodies of the dying.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     For other Greek names beginning with K see C. For example Circe for Kirke, Cronus for Kronos, etc.
    </p>
