    <h1>
     <!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="Title" -->
     NYMPHS
     <!-- InstanceEndEditable -->
    </h1>
    <!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="Menu" -->
    <p>
     The Nymphs (
     <em>
      nymphai
     </em>
     ) were minor nature goddesses which populated the earth. Although they were ranked below the gods, they were still summoned to attend the assemblies of the gods on Olympus.
    </p>
    <p>
     The Nymphs presided over various natural phenomena--from springs, to clouds, trees, caverns, meadows, and beaches. They were responsible for the care of the plants and animals of their domain and as such were closely associated with the Olympian gods of nature such as Hermes, Dionysus, Artemis, Poseidon and Demeter.
    </p>
    <p>
     The male counterparts of the nymphs were the Satyrs, Panes, Potamoi and Tritons.
    </p>

    <h2>
     THE CLASSES OF NYMPH
    </h2>
    <p>
     ANTHUSAE (Anthousai) Nymphs of  flowers. They were probably the same as the Leimenides, a type of Oceanid.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/Aurai.html">
      AURAE
     </a>
     (Aurai) The nymphs of the cooling breezes. They were daughters of the wind-god Boreas, or Okeanos, the earth-encircling fresh-water stream.
    </p>
    <p>
     BACCHAE The Bacchic nymphs, companions of Dionysus. They were also known as Thyiades.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/Epimelides.html">
      BUCOLAE
     </a>
     (Boukolai) Bucolic or rustic nymphs. Another name for the Epimelides.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/Naiades.html">
      CRENAEAE
     </a>
     (Krenaiai) Naiad nymphs of wells and fountains.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/Dryades.html">
      DRYADS
     </a>
     (Dryades) The nymphs of trees and forests. Some of them had their life force bound to that of a specific tree, usually the loftiest in a forest, or one in a sacred grove of the gods. Dryads of mountain pines were known as Oreads, those of ash-trees were called Meliae, Hamadryads were of the oaks, and Meliades  of fruit-trees.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/Epimelides.html">
      EPIMELIDES
     </a>
     (or Epimeliades) Nymphs of highland pasture, the protectresses of sheep-flocks. They were perhaps numbered amongst the Oceanids.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/Haliai.html">
      HALIAE
     </a>
     (Haliai) Nymphs of the sea, the sands, and the rocky shores. They had the schools of fish, and other sea creatures in their keeping. The most famous of them were the fifty Nereids.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/Dryades.html">
      HAMADRYADS
     </a>
     (Hamadryad) Oak-tree Dryads whose life force was bound to that of a particular tree.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/Naiades.html">
      HELEIONOMAE
     </a>
     (Heleionomai) Naiad nymphs of fresh-water marshes and wetlands.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Khthonios/Lampades.html">
      LAMPADES
     </a>
     Torch-bearing nymphs of the underworld. They probably belonged to the train of the goddesses of the Eleusinian Mysteries, Persephone and Hecate.
    </p>
    <p>
     LEIMENIDES Nymphs of  the water-meadows--pastures thick with lush grass and flowers. They were probably a type of Oceanid nymph.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/Naiades.html">
      LIMNATIDES
     </a>
     Naiad nymphs of lakes.
    </p>
    <p>
     MAENADS (Mainades) The frenzied nymphs of the train of Dionysus. Another name for Thyiads.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/Epimelides.html">
      MELIADES
     </a>
     Another name for Epimelides.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiMeliai.html">
      MELIAE
     </a>
     (Meliai) Nymphs of honey, bees and honeydew (manna). They were also Dryads of the mountain ash.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiMeliai.html">
      MELISSAE
     </a>
     (Melissai) Honey nymphs, another name for the Meliae.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/Naiades.html">
      NAIADS
     </a>
     (Naiades) Nymphs of the sources of fresh-water: springs, fountains, streams, rivers, and lakes. They were usually called daughters of Okeanos and the River-Gods.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/Nephelai.html">
      NEPHELAE
     </a>
     (Nephelai) Nymphs of the clouds. They were usually numbered amongst the Oceanids.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Pontios/Nereides.html">
      NEREIDS
     </a>
     (Nereides) Fifty Haliad sea-nymphs.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/Nymphai.html">
      NYMPHS
     </a>
     (Nymphai) Minor goddesses or spirits (
     <em>
      daimonaissai
     </em>
     ) of nature. Many of the classes overlapped: for example, the Dryad nymph of a tree growing by a spring was also often the Naiad of the fountain.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/Okeanides.html">
      OCEANIDS
     </a>
     (Okeanides) Nymphs who presided over the sources of fresh water--both earthly, the streams and fountains, and heavenly, moist breezes and rain-clouds. They were daughers of Oceanus--the earth-encircling, fresh-water stream--and sisters of the River-Gods.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/Dryades.html">
      OREADS
     </a>
     (Oreiades) Nymphs of the mountains. They were Dryad nymphs, whose life force was closely tied to that of a lofty mountain pine or fir.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/Naiades.html">
      PEGAEAE
     </a>
     (Pegaiai) Naiad nymphs of springs.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/Naiades.html">
      POTAMEIDES
     </a>
     Nymphs of the rivers. They were a type of Naiad.
    </p>
    <p>
     THYIADS (Thyiades) Wild, orgiastic nymphs of the train of the god Dionysus. They were also known as Maenads, Bacchae and Bacchantes. The Thyiad troupe consisted of a mixture of Dryad and Naiad nymphs.
    </p>

    </p>

      ACHEIA
     </a>
     (Akheia) One of the Hyperborean nymphs, handmaidens of the goddess Artemis from the mythical Northern land of Hyperborea. She was usually named Hecaerge.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiAkheloides.html">
      ACHELOIDES
     </a>
     (Akheloides) Naiads of the River Achelous in Aetolia, central Greece.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiAsterionides.html">
      ACRAEA
     </a>
     (Akraia) One of the three Asterionides, nymphs of the River Asterion in Argos, the Peloponnesos, southern Greece. She was a nurse of the goddess Hera.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Pontios/Nereides.html">
      ACTAEA
     </a>
     (Aktaie) One of the fifty Nereids.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/Okeanides.html">
      ADMETE
     </a>
     One of the Oceanid nymphs.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiIdaiai.html">
      ADRASTEIA
     </a>
     One of the nymphs of Mount Ida on the island of Crete in the Greek Aegean. She a nurse of the god Zeus.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheAia.html">
      AEA
     </a>
     (Aia) The Naiad nymph of the town of Aea (or Colchis) near the Caucasus Mountains on the Black Sea. She was loved the river-god Phasis.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiAiaiai.html">
      "AEAEAEN" NYMPHS
     </a>
     (Nymphai Aiaiai) Naiads and Dryads of the mythical island of Aeaea. They were handmaidens of the goddess Circe.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiAigaiides.html">
      "AEGAEIDES"
     </a>
     (Aigaiides) Naiads of the River Aegaeus of the mythical island of the Phaeacians. The island was sometimes identified with Greek Corcyra.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiHamadryades.html">
      AEGEIROS
     </a>
     (Aigeiros) One of the eight Hamadryad nymphs. Her tree was the black poplar.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheAigina.html">
      AEGINA
     </a>
     (Aigina) The Naiad nymph of the main town of the island of Aegina Island, southern Greece. She was abducted from her father's mainland stream by Zeus in the shape of an eagle.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheAigle.html">
      AEGLE (1)
     </a>
     (Aigle) The nymph mother of the Charites by the sun-god Helius. She was probably one of the Oceanids.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiHeliades.html">
      AEGLE (2)
     </a>
     (Aigle) One of the Heliad nymphs who were transformed into amber-weeping poplar trees.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiAisepides.html">
      AESEPIDES
     </a>
     (Aisepides) Naiads of the River Aisepos in the Troad, Anatolia (modern Turkey).
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiHeliades.html">
      AETHERIE
     </a>
     One of the Heliad nymphs who were transformed into amber-weeping poplar trees.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphePleione.html">
      AETHRA
     </a>
     (Aithre) The Oceanid nymph wife of the Titan Atlas. She was usually named Pleione.
    </p>
    <p>
     AETHUSA (Aithousa) A nymph daughter of Poseidon and the Pleaid Alcyone.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheAix.html">
      AEX (1)
     </a>
     (Aix) The nymph wife of the god Pan. She was also seduced by Zeus.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiIdaiai.html">
      AEX (2)
     </a>
     (Aix) One of the nymphs of Mount Ida on the island of Crete in the Greek Aegean. She was a nurse of the god Zeus. Aix was usually called Amaltheia and described as a goat.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheAganippe.html">
      AGANIPPE
     </a>
     The Naiad nymph of the spring of Aganippe on Mount Helicon in Boeotia, central Greece. Her waters were sacred to the Muses.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Pontios/Nereides.html">
      AGAVE
     </a>
     (Agaue) One of the fifty Nereids.
    </p>
    <p>
     ALCE (Alke) A Naiad nymph of the River Sangarius in Phrygia, Anatolia (modern Turkey).
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiLykaiai.html">
      ALCINOE
     </a>
     (Alkinoe) A Naiad nymph of Mount Lycaeus in Arcadia, the Peloponnese, central Greece. She was one of nine Arcadian nurses of the god Zeus.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiAlkyonides.html">
      ALCIPPE
     </a>
     (Alkippe) One of the nymphs known as Alcyonides.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheAlkyone.html">
      ALCYONE
     </a>
     (Alkyone) The Pleiad nymph of Boeotia, central Greece. She was loved by the god Poseidon.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiAlkyonides.html">
      ALCYONIDES
     </a>
     (Alkyonides) Seven nymphs of the Thracian Chersonese (modern north Greece). They were daughters of the Gigante Alcyoneus who were transformed by the gods into kingfishers.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheAlexirhoe.html">
      ALEXIRHOE
     </a>
     A Naiad nymph of the springs of the River Grenicus on Mount Ida in the Troad, Anatolia (modern Turkey). She was loved by King Priam of Troy.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiIdaiai.html">
      ALTHAEA
     </a>
     (Althaia) One of the nymphs of Mount Ida on the island of Crete in the Greek Aegean. She a nurse of the god Zeus. Althaea was usually named Amaltheia and described as a goat.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiIdaiai.html">
      AMALTHEIA
     </a>
     One of the nymphs of Mount Ida on the island of Crete in the Greek Aegean. She a nurse of the god Zeus. Amaltheia was usually described as the milk-providing goat of the nurses, rather than a nymph herself.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Pontios/Nereides.html">
      AMATHEIA
     </a>
     One of the fifty Nereids.
    </p>
    <p>

      AMBROSIA (1)

    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphePronoia.html">
      PRONOEA
     </a>
     (Pronoia) An Oceanid nymph of Mount Parnassus in Phocis, central Greece. She was the wife of the Titan Prometheus.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiAsterionides.html">
      PROSYMNA
     </a>
     One of the three Asterionides, nymphs of the River Asterion in Argolis in the Peloponnese. She was a nurse of the goddess Hera.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Pontios/Nereides.html">
      PROTO
     </a>
     One of the fifty Nereids.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Pontios/Nereides.html">
      PROTOMEDEIA
     </a>
     One of the fifty Nereids.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/Okeanides.html">
      PRYMNO
     </a>
     One of the Oceanid nymphs.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphePsalakantha.html">
      PSALACANTHA
     </a>
     (Psalakantha) A nymph of the island of Icaria in the Greek Aegean. She was spurned by the god Dionysus and so tried to dissuade Ariadne from consorting with the god. As punishment he transormed her into the plany-plant.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Pontios/NereisPsamathe.html">
      PSAMATHE
     </a>
     One of the fifty Nereids. She was the wife of the sea-god Proteus and loved by King Aeacus of AEgina.
    </p>
    <p>
     PSANIS The Naiad nymph of an Arcadian spring. She may have been the wife of the River Ladon.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiArtemisiai.html">
      PSECAS
     </a>
     (Psekas) One of the Oceanids in the retinue of the goddess Artemis.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiHamadryades.html">
      PTELEA
     </a>
     One of the eight Hamadryad nymphs. Her tree was the elm.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiArtemisiai.html">
      RHANIS
     </a>
     One of the Oceanid nymph companions of the goddess Artemis.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheRhene.html">
      RHENE
     </a>
     A nymph of the island of Samothrace in the Greek Aegean. She was loved by the god Hermes (or perhaps Zeus).
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheRhetia.html">
      RHETIA
     </a>
     A nymph of the island of Samothrace in the Greek Aegean. She was the mother of the Samothracian Corybantes by Apollo.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheRhode.html">
      RHODE
     </a>
     The Haliad nymph of the island of Rhodes in the Greek Aegean. She was the wife of the sun-god Helius.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/Okeanides.html">
      RHODEIA
     </a>
     One of the Oceanid nymphs.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheRhodope.html">
      RHODOPE (1)
     </a>
     A Naiad nymph of Ciconia in Thrace, north of Greece. She and her arrogant husband Haemus were transformed into mountains as punishment for pretending to be Zeus and Hera.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/Okeanides.html">
      RHODOPE (2)
     </a>
     One of the Oceanid nymphs.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheRhode.html">
      RHODOS
     </a>
     The Haliad nymph of the island of Rhodes in the Greek Aegean. She was usually named Rhode.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheStrymo.html">
      RHOEO
     </a>
     (Rhoio) A Naiad nymph of the town of Troy in the Troad, Anatolia (modern Turkey). She was the wife of the Trojan King Laomedon. She was usually named Strymo.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiRhyndakides.html">
      "RHYNDACIDES"
     </a>
     (Rhyndakides) Naiads of the springs of the River Rhyndacus in Mysia, Anatolia (modern Turkey).
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheSagaritis.html">
      SAGARITIS
     </a>
     A Naiad and Hamadryad nymph of the River Sangarius in Phrygia, Anatolia (modern Turkey). She had an affair with Attis, consort of Cybele, and the goddess felled her tree in retribution.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheSalamis.html">
      SALAMIS
     </a>
     The Naiad nymph of the main town of the island of Salamis in southern Greece. She was abducted to the island by the god Poseidon.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheSalmakis.html">
      SALMACIS
     </a>
     (Salmakis) The Naiad nymph of the spring Salmacis of the town of Halicarnassus in Caria, Anatolia (modern Turkey). She fell in love with the boy Hermaphroditus and prayed to the gods to be with him forever. The gods then merged their forms to create the first hermaphrodite.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheSamia.html">
      SAMIA
     </a>
     The Naiad nymph of the main town of the island of Samos in the Greek Aegean. She was the wife of the island's first king Ancaeus.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Pontios/Nereides.html">
      SAO
     </a>
     One of the fifty Nereids.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheSatnioeis.html">
      "SATNIOEIAN" NAIAD
     </a>
     (Naias Satnioeis) An unnamed Naiad nymph of the River Satnioeis in the Troad, Anatolia (modern Turkey). She was loved by the Dardanian prince Enops.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Pontios/Skylla.html">
      SCYLLA
     </a>
     (Skylla) A Sicilian sea-nymph who was transformed into a monster by the witch Circe.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheSeriphia.html">
      "SERIPHIAN" NAIAD
     </a>
     (Naias Seriphia) An unnamed Naiad of the island of Seriphos in the Greek Aegean. She was loved by King Magnes of Magnesia but emigrated with her two sons to the island.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheSinoe.html">
      SINOE
     </a>
     An Oread nymph of Mount Sinoe in Arcadia, southern Greece. She was the nurse of the god Pan.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheSinope.html">
      SINOPE
     </a>
     The Naiad nymph of the Greek colony of Sinope in Assyria on the Black Sea, Anatolia (modern Turkey). She was carried off to the land by Zeus but had him swear an oath to protect her virginity. The River-God Halys and Apollo also failed to win her.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Pontios/Seirenes.html">
      SIRENS
     </a>
     (Seirenes) Three Naiad nymph companions of Persephone. Demeter gave them the bodies of birds to help with the search for her abducted daughter. They failed in this endeavour and settled on the island of Anthemoessa where they lured passing sailors to their deaths with an irresistable song.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiSithnides.html">
      SITHNIDES
     </a>
     Naiads of the fountain of the town of Megara in Attica, southern Greece.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheSose.html">
      SOSE
     </a>
     A prophetic Oread nymph of the region of Arcadia in southern Greece. She was the mother of the god Pan by Hermes.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheSparta.html">
      SPARTA
     </a>
     (Sparte) The Naiad nymph of the town of Sparta in Lacedaemonia, southern Greece. She was the wife of King Lacedaemon.
    </p>

     <a href="../Pontios/Nereides.html">
      SPEIO

    </p>

     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiSphragitides.html">

     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheThisbe.html">

     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiThriai.html">

    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheThronie.html">
      THRONIA

     (Thronie) The Naiad nymph of the town of Abdera in Bistonia in Thrace, north of Greece. She was loved by the god Poseidon.

     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheThyia.html">

     A Naiad nymph of the shrine of Delphi in Phocis, central Greece. She was loved by the god Apollo.

     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheThymbris.html">
