    <h1>

     GREEK GODS FAMILY TREE
     <!-- InstanceEndEditable -->
    </h1>
    <!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="Menu" -->
    <p>
     The family tree of the ancient Greek gods is depicted here in a large series of charts which include a short summary of the main gods, an illustration of Hesiod's
     <em>
      Theogony
     </em>
     , and a comprehensive eight-part set sourced from a broad selection of ancient texts.
     <br/>
     There are two versions of each chart--one with the common English name-spellings and a second with precise transliterations of the ancient Greek names.
    </p>

    <h2>
     (3) DETAILED FAMILY CHARTS
    </h2>
    <p>
     The complete family tree of the gods is displayed over eight indexed charts. The basic structure follows Hesiod's
     <em>
      Theogony
     </em>
     , but that author's genealogies have been expanded with a plethora of additional gods, spirits and creatures sourced from other classical sources. Where there is disagreement amongst ancient writers as to the genealogy of a certain character, the oldest and/or most popular source has been selected for the chart.
    </p>
    <p>
     An additional family tree depicts the divine genealogy given in Hesiod's
     <em>
      Theogony
     </em>
     .
    </p>
    <p>
     Click on any name in the chart to view the full page entry for that individual.
    </p>

The spirits and sea-gods stand bottom left and centre. The background is from a C6th B.C. Athenian black figure vase painting.
