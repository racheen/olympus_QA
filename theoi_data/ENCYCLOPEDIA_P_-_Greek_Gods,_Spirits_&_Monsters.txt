     </a>
     The king of the sea, and god of horses, rivers and earthquakes. He was one of the twelve Olympian gods who received the sea as his domain when the three sons of Cronus drew lots for the division of the cosmos after the fall of the Titans.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/Naiades.html">
      POTAMEIDES
     </a>
     Naiad-nymphs of rivers.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Potamos/Potamoi.html">
      POTAMOI
     </a>
     The river-god sons of Oceanus and Tethys. They were mutiformed deities--some had the shape of man-headed bulls, others were merman-like creatures with the upper bodies of men and the serpentine tails of fish.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ouranios/ErosPothos.html">
      POTHUS
     </a>
     (Pothos) The god of sexual longing. He was one of the winged Erotes (Love-Gods).
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Daimon/Praxidikai.html">
      PRAXIDICAE
     </a>
     (Praxidikai) The goddess exacters of justice.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Daimon/Praxidike.html">
      PRAXIDICE
     </a>
     (Praxidike) The goddess exacter of justice.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphePraxithea.html">
      PRAXITHEA
     </a>
     The Naiad-nymph wife of King Erechtheus of Athens.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Georgikos/Priapos.html">
      PRIAPUS
     </a>
     (Priapos) The rustic god of garden fertility. He was an ugly satry-like creature with oversized genitals.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Daimon/Proioxis.html">
      PROIOXIS
     </a>
     The battlefield personification of onrush.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Titan/TitanPrometheus.html">
      PROMETHEUS
     </a>
     The Titan-god of forethought who was tasked with the creation of mankind. He cheated the gods on several occasions including the theft of fire. As punishment Zeus chained him to Mount Caucasus and set an eagle to feed on his ever-regenerating liver. He later freed from his torments by Heracles.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphePronoia.html">
      PRONOEA
     </a>
     (Pronoia) The Oceanid-goddess of foresight, wife of the Titan Prometheus.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Georgikos/SatyroiHermeides.html">
      PRONOMUS
     </a>
     (Pronomos) One of the three Satyr messengers of the god Dionysus.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Daimon/Prophasis.html">
      PROPHASIS
     </a>
     The female personification of excuse, daughter of the Titan Epimetheus (Afterthought).
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiAsterionides.html">
      PROSYMNA
     </a>
     A Naiad-nymph daughter of the river Asterion. She and her sisters Euboea and Acraea were nurses of the infant goddess Hera.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Pontios/Proteus.html">
      PROTEUS
     </a>
     An old sea-god who herded the seals of Poseidon. He was once captured by the hero Menelaus who held him fast and forced him to prophesy.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="primeval-gods.html">
      PROTOGENOI
     </a>
     The first-born, primordial gods which formed the very fabric of the cosmos--Chaos (Air), Gaea (Earth), Tartarus (The Pit Below), Uranus (Sky), Pontus (Sea), Nyx (Night), Erebus (Darkness), Hemera (Day), and Aether (Light). Each had the form of his or her own element, though a few were known to appear in anthropomorphic form.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphePsalakantha.html">
      PSALACANTHA
     </a>
     (Psalakantha) An Icarian nymph who fell in love with the god Dionysus. When she attempted to dissuade Ariadne from marrying the god, he angrily transformed her into a plany-plant.
    </p>
    <figure class="image-main image-main-400">
     <a href="../Ouranios/Psykhe.html">
      <img alt="Psyche" class="img-responsive center-block" src="../image/Z31.1Eros.jpg"/>
     </a>
     <figcaption>
      <a href="../Ouranios/Psykhe.html">
       PSYCHE
      </a>
     </figcaption>
    </figure>
    <p>
     <a href="../Pontios/NereisPsamathe.html">
      PSAMATHE
     </a>
     The Nereid-goddess of sand who was seduced by King Aeacus of Aegina.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiArtemisiai.html">
      PSECAS
     </a>
     (Psekas) One of the attendant nymphs of the goddess Artemis.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Daimon/Pseudologoi.html">
      PSEUDOLOGOI
     </a>
     The male personifications of lies.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ouranios/Psykhe.html">
      PSYCHE
     </a>
     (Psykhe) The goddess of the soul, wife of the love-god Eros (Cupid). She was originally a mortal princess who rivalled Aphrodite in beauty. When Eros fell in love with her the goddess put her through many trials before accepting her into the Olympian fold.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiHamadryades.html">
      PTELEA
     </a>
     The Hamadryad-nymph of the elm tree.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Daimon/Ptokheia.html">
      PTOCHEIA
     </a>
     (Ptokheia) The female personification of beggary.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Phylos/Pygmaioi.html">
      PYGMIES
     </a>
     (Pygmaioi) A tribe of two-foot tall men of the far south who waged war on the migrating flocks of cranes.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Khthonios/PotamosPyriphlegethon.html">
      PYRIPHLEGETHON
     </a>
     The underworld river of fire and its god.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Georgikos/KouretePyrrhikhos.html">
      PYRRHICHUS
     </a>
     (Pyrrhikhos) The Curete-god of the
     <em>
      pyrrhichus
     </em>
     dance.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Titan/AsterPyroeis.html">
      PYROEIS
     </a>
     The star-god of the planet Mars.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ther/DrakainaPython.html">
      PYTHON
     </a>
     A giant serpent born of the sludge of the earth which was set by Themis or Gaea to guard the oracle of Delphi. Apollo slew the beast with arrows when he claimed the shrine.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
