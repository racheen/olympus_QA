    <h2>
     OVERVIEW OF THE BESTIARY
    </h2>
    <p>
     The fantastic creatures of Greek mythology and legend can be divided into eight broad categories :
    </p>
    <p>
     1. MYTHICAL MONSTERS
    </p>
    <p>
     Awful humanoid creatures. These included monsters such as the Gorgons and Echidna.
    </p>
    <p>
     2. MYTHICAL HYBRIDS
    </p>
    <p>
     Creatures which combine human and animal forms or various animals. These include creatures such as the Centaur (Horse-Man), Sphinx (Lion-Woman), Hippocamp (Fish-Horse), and Chimaera (Lion-Goat-Snake).
    </p>
    <p>
     3. MYTHICAL ANIMALS
    </p>
    <p>
     Normal animals with some fantastic attribute. Examples include the winged horse Pegasus, the giant Erymanthian Boar, the fire-breathing Bulls of Colchis, and the inescapable hunting-dog Laelaps.
    </p>
    <p>
     4.
     <a href="dragons.html">
      DRAGONS
     </a>
    </p>
    <p>
     A breed of serpentine monsters, usually equipped with some magical power. They include creatures such as the Hydra, the Dragon of the Golden Fleece, Python, the Ethiopian Sea-Monster and the she-dragon Echidna.
    </p>
    <p>
     5.
     <a href="giants.html">
      GIANTS
     </a>
    </p>
    <p>
     Gigantic men, often with fantastic features or abilities. A few examples are the hundred-handed Hecatoncheires, the one-eyed Cyclopes, the water-walking hunter Orion, the wrestler Antaeus, and the monstrous Typhon.
    </p>
    <p>
     6. GHOSTS &amp; DEMONS
    </p>
    <p>
     Creatures from the underworld which haunted the earth. They included the Empusae, Erinyes, Vampires and haunting ghosts of the dead.
    </p>
    <p>
     7.
     <a href="fantastic-creatures.html">
      LEGENDARY CREATURES
     </a>
    </p>
    <p>
     Fantastic animals which were believed to inhabit the remote corners of the earth. They included the Manticore, Unicorn (Greek Monoceratus), Catoblepas, and Griffin.
    </p>
    <p>
     8.
     <a href="fabulous-tribes.html">
      LEGENDARY TRIBES
     </a>
    </p>
    <p>
     Fabulous tribes of men which were believed to inhabit the lands of
     <em>
      Terra Incognita
     </em>
     , the "Unknown Earth." Examples of these are the Pygmies, the one-legged Sciapods, dog-headed Cynocephali, headless Blemmyae, and one-eyed Arimaspians.
    </p>

    <h2>
     BESTIARY
    </h2>
    <figure class="image-main image-main-400">
     <a href="../Pontios/Gorgones.html">
      <img alt="Medusa" class="img-responsive center-block" src="../image/P23.1BMedousa.jpg"/>
     </a>
     <figcaption>
      <a href="../Pontios/Gorgones.html">
       MEDUSA
      </a>
     </figcaption>
    </figure>
    <p>
     Greek myth and legend is filled with a wide variety of monsters and creatures ranging from Dragons, Giants, Demons and Ghosts, to multiformed creatures such as the Sphinx, Minotaur, Centaurs, Manticores and Griffins. There were also many fabulous animals such as the Nemean Lion, golden-fleeced Ram and winged horse Pegasus, not to mention the creatures of legend such as the Phoenix, Unicorns (Monocerata). Even amongst the tribes of man, myth spoke of strange peoples inhabiting the far reaches of the earth such as the  hopping Umbrella-Foots, the one-eyed Arimaspians, the Dog-Headed men, and the puny Pygmies.
    </p>
    <p>
     AMPHISBAENAE see Beasts, African
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ther/Automotones.html">
      AUTOMOTONS
     </a>
     (Automotones) Creatures crafted out of metal and endowed with life by the smith-god Hephaestus.
    </p>
    <ul>
     <li>
      <a href="../Ther/TauroiKhalkeoi.html">
       BULLS, BRONZE
      </a>
      (Tauroi Khalkeoi) Four fire-breathing, bronze bulls which Hephaestus crafted for King Aeetes of Colchis.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Ther/AutomotonesKeledones.html">
       CELEDONES
      </a>
      (Keledones) Golden singing maidens which Hephaistos crafted for the first temple of Apollo at Delphi.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Ther/Automotones.html#Kuon">
       DOGS, GOLD &amp; SILVER
      </a>
      (Kuones Khryseos Argyreos) A pair of gold and silver dogs which Hephaestus crafted for King Alcinous of the Phaeacians.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Ther/HippoiKabeirikoi.html">
       HORSES, CABEIRIAN
      </a>
      (Hippoi Kabeirikoi) A pair of metallic, fire-breathing horses owned by the twin Cabiri gods.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Ther/AutomotonesKourai.html">
       MAIDENS, GOLDEN
      </a>
      (Kourai Khryseai) Four golden maidens which Hephaestus crafted as his own attendants.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Gigante/GiganteTalos.html">
       TALOS
      </a>
      A bronze giant which Hephaestus crafted for Queen Europa of Crete to patrol the borders of her island.
     </li>
    </ul>
    <p>
     BASILISCS see Beasts, African
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Thaumasios/TheresAithiopikoi.html">
      BEASTS, AFRICAN
     </a>
     (Theres Aithiopes) The semi-legendary land of Ethiopia (Sub-saharan Africa) was the home of many fabulous beasts.
    </p>
    <ul>
     <li>
      <a href="../Thaumasios/Amphisbainai.html">
       AMPHISBAENAE
      </a>
      (Amphisbainai) Saharan snakes with two heads, one at each end of the body.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Thaumasios/Basiliskoi.html">
       BASILISCS
      </a>
      (Basiliskoi) Deadly serpents which killed by touch.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Thaumasios/TauroiAithiopikoi.html">
       BULLS, ETHIOPIAN
      </a>
      (Tauroi Aithiopikoi) Gigantic African bulls whose red hides were impervious to steel.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Thaumasios/Katoblepones.html">
       CATOBLEPAS
      </a>
      (Katoblepon)  African hoofed animals whose downward looking head, when raised, could kill man with a gaze or  with its noxious breath.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Thaumasios/DrakonesAithiopikoi.html">
       DRAGONS, ETHIOPIAN
      </a>
      (Drakones Aithiopikoi) Gigantic African serpents.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Thaumasios/Leukrokotai.html">
       LEUCROCOTAE
      </a>
      (Leukrokota) An animals with a powerful jaw of bone in place of teeth, which could imitate human voices to lure it prey.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Thaumasios/PegasoiAithiopikoi.html">
       PEGASI, ETHIOPIAN
      </a>
      (Pegasoi Aithiopikoi) The winged horses of Ethiopia who had a single horn growing from their forehead.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Thaumasios/SatyroiNesioi.html">
       SATYRS, ISLAND
      </a>
      (Satyroi Nesioi) Ape-like satyrs native to certain islands off the African coast.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Thaumasios/SatyroiLibyes.html">
       SATYRS, LIBYAN
      </a>
      (Satyroi Libyes) Monkey-like satyrs who lived in the forest of Mount Atlas.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Thaumasios/SphinxesAithiopikoi.html">
       SPHINXES, ETHIOPIAN
      </a>
      (Sphinxes Aithiopikoi) Women-headed African lions.
     </li>
     <figure class="image-main image-main-400">
      <a href="../Ther/Minotauros.html">
       <img alt="Minotaur" class="img-responsive center-block" src="../image/T34.4Minotauros.jpg"/>
      </a>
      <figcaption>
       <a href="../Ther/Minotauros.html">
        MINOTAUR
       </a>
      </figcaption>
     </figure>
    </ul>
    <p>
     <a href="../Thaumasios/TheresIndikoi.html">
      BEASTS, INDIAN
     </a>
     (Theres Indikoi) Legendary India was the home of many fabulous beasts.
    </p>
    <ul>
     <li>
      <a href="../Thaumasios/MyrmekesIndikoi.html">
       ANTS, INDIAN
      </a>
      (Myrmekes Indikoi) Gigantic ants which guarded the gold-fields of the Indian desert.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Thaumasios/KeteaIndikoi.html">
       CETEA, INDIAN
      </a>
      (Ketea Indikoi) Fabulous half-animal, half-fish sea-monster such as fish-tailed lions, bears, wolves, rams and even spiny-haired mermaids.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Thaumasios/DrakonesIndikoi.html">
       DRAGONS, INDIAN
      </a>
      (Drakones Indikoi) Giant Indian serpents which preyed on elephants.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Thaumasios/HippoiMonokerata.html">
       MONOCERATA
      </a>
      (Hippoi Monokerata) The unicorns, magical single-horned equines native to India.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Thaumasios/WormsIndos.html">
       WORMS, INDOS
      </a>
      Gigantic white worms which inhabited the Indos river.
     </li>
    </ul>
    <p>
     BEASTS, PERSIAN &amp; ARABIAN (Theres Persikoi) Various fabulous animals were believed to inhabit the lands of Arabia and Persia. See also Manticore and Phoenix.
    </p>
    <ul>
     <li>
      <a href="../Thaumasios/OphiesPteretoi.html">
       SERPENTS, WINGED
      </a>
      (Ophies Pteretoi) Feathery-winged serpents of Arabia which guarded the valuable myrrh fields.
     </li>
    </ul>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ther/OrnithesAreioi.html">
      BIRDS, OF ARES
     </a>
     (Ornithes Areioi) Arrow-shooting birds which guarded the Amazonian shrine of the god Ares.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ther/OrnithesStymphalides.html">
      BIRDS, STYMPHALIAN
     </a>
     (Ornithes Stymphalides) Man-eating which haunted lake Stymphalus in Arkadia. Heracles weas sent to drive them away as one of his Labours
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ther/HusKalydonios.html">
      BOAR, CALYDONIAN
     </a>
     (Hus Kalydonios) A gigantic Aetolian boar slain by a band of heroes in the famed Calydonian Boar Hunt.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ther/HusKlazomenaios.html">
      BOAR, CLAZOMENEAN
     </a>
     (Hus Klazomenaios) A gigantic winged sow which ravaged the island of Clazomenae.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ther/HusKrommyon.html">
      BOAR, CROMMYONIAN
     </a>
     (Hus Krommyon) A gigantic sow which ravaged the region of Crommyon. It was slain by the hero Theseus.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ther/HusErymanthios.html">
      BOAR, ERYMANTHIAN
     </a>
     (Hus Erymanthios) A gigantic Arcadian boar which Heracles was sent to fetch as one of his twelve labours.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ther/TaurosKretaios.html">
      BULL, CRETAN
     </a>
     (Tauros Kretaios) The bull which impregnated Pasiphae queen of Crete and which Heracles was sent to fetch as one of his labours.
    </p>
    <p>
     BULL, OF EUROPA (Tauros Europaios) A bull which carried the Phoenician princess Europa across the sea to Crete. It was sometimes depicted as a fish-tailed animal.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ther/TaurosOphis.html">
      BULL, SERPENT
     </a>
     (Tauros Ophis) A black-skinned monster with the foreparts of a bull and the rearparts of a serpent. It was slain by the Sea-Titan Aegaeon during the Titan-War.
    </p>
    <p>
     CATOBLEPAS see Beasts, African
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Georgikos/KentauroiThessalioi.html">
      CENTAURS
     </a>
     (Kentauroi) A tribe of creatures who had the heads and torsos of men and the bodies of horses.
    </p>
    <ul>
     <li>
      <a href="../Georgikos/Kentaurides.html">
       CENTAURIDES
      </a>
      (Kentaurides) The female centaurs, women with the bodies of horses in place of legs.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Georgikos/KentauroiKyprioi.html">
       CENTAURS, CYPRIAN
      </a>
      (Kentauroi Kyprioi) A tribe of centaurs native to the island of Cyprus.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Georgikos/PheresLamioi.html">
       CENTAURS, DIONYSIAN
      </a>
      (Kentauroi Lamoi) The centaur attendants of the god Dionysos.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Georgikos/KentauroiPeloponnesioi.html">
       CENTAURS, PELOPONNESIAN
      </a>
      (Kentauroi Peloponnesioi) A tribe of Arcadian centaurs which battled Heracles for the wine of Pholus.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Georgikos/KentauroiThessalioi.html">
       CENTAURS, THESSALIAN
      </a>
      (Kentauroi Thessalioi) A tribe of Magnesian centaurs who battled the Lapith tribesmen at the wedding of King Pirithoos.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Georgikos/KentaurosKheiron.html">
       CHIRON
      </a>
      (Kheiron) A wise, immortal Centaur, half-brother of Zeus. He was the mentor of many Greek heroes.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Georgikos/KentaurosEurytion1.html">
       EURYTION 1
      </a>
      A Thessalian centaur who tried to abduct the bride of King Pirithoos.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Georgikos/KentaurosEurytion2.html">
       EURYTION  2
      </a>
      A Pelopennesian centaur slain by Heracles.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Pontios/Ikhthyokentauroi.html">
       ICHTHYOCENTAURS
      </a>
      (Ikhthyokentauroi) Two marine centaurs, named Bythos and Aphros, with the upper torsoes of a men and the lower bodies of hippocamps (fish-tailed horses).
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Georgikos/KentaurosNessos.html">
       NESSUS
      </a>
      (Nessos) A centaur slain by Heracles.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Georgikos/KentaurosPholos.html">
       PHOLUS
      </a>
      (Pholos) A goodly centaur who entertained Heracles.
     </li>
    </ul>

    <p>
     <a href="../Ther/KuonKerberos.html">
      CERBERUS
     </a>
     (Kerberos) The three-headed, giant hound that guarded the gates of Hades. It had lion's feet, a mane of serpents and the tail of a viper.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Georgikos/Kerkopes.html">

      <a href="../Phasma/PhasmaPhilinnion.html">
       GHOST, PHILINNION
      </a>
      A dead Macedonian woman who rose up from her tomb at night to haunt a handsome young guest of her parents. She was probably a lamia (vampire).
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Phasma/PhasmaPolykritos.html">
       GHOST, POLYCRITUS
      </a>
      (Polykritos) A dead man who rose up from his grave and devoured his living hermaphroditic son.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Phasma/Empousai.html">
       LAMIAE
      </a>
      (Lamiai) Vampiric demons whichy preyed on young men in the guise of a beautiful woman and drank their blood.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Phasma/LamiaKorinthia.html">
       LAMIA CORINTHIAN
      </a>
      (Lamia Korinthia) A vampiric Lamia who disguised herself as a wealthy Phoenician woman in order to seduce a handsome young man and drain his blood.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Ther/Lamia.html">
       LAMIA LIBYAN
      </a>
      (Lamia Libys) A vampiric Libyan demon which preyed on children. She could pluck out her own bleeding eyes.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Phasma/PhasmaEidolones.html">
       PHANTOMS, EIDOLONES
      </a>
      Evil man-possessing spirits or ghosts.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Phasma/PhasmaEphesios.html">
       PHANTOM, EPHESIAN PLAGUE
      </a>
      (Eidos Loimos Ephesios) A plague-bringing demon that haunted the town of Ephesus.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Phasma/PhasmaTaraxippoi.html">
       TARAXIPPI
      </a>
      (Taraxippoi) Horse-frightening ghosts or daemones which haunted the race-courses of Olympia, Nemea and the Isthmus.
     </li>
    </ul>
    <p>
     <a href="../Thaumasios/Phoinix.html">
      PHOENIX
     </a>
     (Phoinix) A fabulous golden-red bird whose feathers shone with the light of the sun.
    </p>
    <p>
     PYGMIES See Men, fantastic
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ther/KriosKhrysomallos.html">
      RAM, GOLDEN-FLEECED
     </a>
     (Krios Khrysomallos) A flying, talking, golden-fleeced Ram who rescued the children Phrixus and Helle from a sacrifice to the gods.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Potamos/Potamoi.html">
      RIVER GODS &amp; DAEMONES
     </a>
     (Theoi Daimones Potamoi) The river-gods appeared either as men from the chest upwards, set upon the serpentine tail of a fish and crowned with a single bull's horn, or as horned man-headed bulls.
    </p>
    <ul>
     <li>
      <a href="../Potamos/PotamosAkheloios.html">
       ACHELOUS
      </a>
      (Akheloios) An Aetolian river-god who was wrestled by Heracles.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Titan/TitanOkeanos.html">
       OCEANUS
      </a>
      (Okeanos) The god of the great earth-encircling river Ocean. He had the tail of a serpentine fish and a bull's horn on his head.
     </li>
    </ul>
    <figure class="image-main image-main-400">
     <a href="../Gallery/O21.3.html">
      <img alt="Sirens" class="img-responsive center-block" src="../image/O21.3BSeirenes.jpg"/>
     </a>
     <figcaption>
      <a href="../Titan/Selene.html">
       SIRENS
      </a>
     </figcaption>
    </figure>
    <p>
     <a href="../Georgikos/Satyroi.html">
      SATYRS
     </a>
     (Satyroi) A tribe of nature-spirits with the body of men, the tails of horses, pug noses and the ears of asses.
    </p>
    <ul>
     <li>
      <a href="../Georgikos/SatyrosMarsyas.html">
       MARSYAS
      </a>
      A satyr defeated by Apollo in a musical contest.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Thaumasios/SatyrosAithiopikos.html">
       SATYR, ETHIOPIAN
      </a>
      (Satyros Aithiopikos) A phantom satyr which plagued an African town.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Thaumasios/SatyrosLemnios.html">
       SATYR, LEMNIAN
      </a>
      (Satyros Lemnios) A satyr which consorted with a woman of the island of Lemnos.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Georgikos/Seilenoi.html">
       SILENI
      </a>
      (Seilenoi) A race of elderly Satyrs with the bodies of old-men, pug-noses, the tails of horses, and asses' ears.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Georgikos/Seilenos.html">
       SILENUS
      </a>
      (Seilenos) An elderly Satyr companion of Dionysus.
     </li>
    </ul>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ther/Skorpios.html">
      SCORPION
     </a>
     (Skorpios) A giant scorpion sent by Gaea to slay the giant Orion.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="sea-gods.html">
      SEA GODS &amp; SEA DAEMONS
     </a>
     (Daimones Einalioi, Theoi Einalioi) Many of sea-gods appeared as fish-tailed mermen, or were otherwise fabulous in form.
    </p>
    <ul>
     <li>
      <a href="../Pontios/Kharybdis.html">
       CHARYBDIS
      </a>
      (Kharybdis) A sea daemon whose inhalations formed a deadly whirlpool.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Ther/Delphin.html">
       DELPHIN
      </a>
      (Delphin) An immortal dolphin attendant of Poseidon.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Titan/TitanisEurynome.html">
       EURYNOME
      </a>
      A mermaid-like sea-goddess with the tail of a fish in place of legs.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Pontios/Glaukos.html">
       GLAUCUS
      </a>
      (Glaukos) A blue-skinned, green-haired, fish-tailed sea-god.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Pontios/Nereus.html">
       NEREUS
      </a>
      An elderly sea-god with the coiled tail of a fish in place of legs.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Pontios/Skylla.html">
       SCYLLA
      </a>
      (Skylla) A monstrous sea-dracaena with the upper body of a beautiful nymphe, the serpentine-tail of a sea-monster and sprung from her waist a ring of six dog foreparts.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Pontios/Telkhines.html">
       TELCHINES
      </a>
      (Telkhines) Sea-demons with the heads of dogs and flippers of seals in place of hands.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Pontios/Triton.html">
       TRITON
      </a>
      A sea-god with either a single or a pair of serpentine fish-tails in place of legs.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Pontios/Tritones.html">
       TRITONES
      </a>
      A race of sea daemones with the upper bodies of men and the tails of fish. Some were fearsome, scaly monsters with sharp carnivore teeth.
     </li>
    </ul>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ther/Ketea.html">
      SEA MONSTERS
     </a>
     (Ketea) A race of gigantic, serpentine sea-dragons.
    </p>
    <ul>
     <li>
      <a href="../Thaumasios/Skolopendra.html">
       SCOLOPENDRA
      </a>
      (Skolopendra) A gargantuan sea-monster with hair extending from its nostrils, a flat crayfish-like tail and rows of webbed feet lining each of its flanks.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Ther/KetosAithiopios.html">
       SEA MONSTER, ETHIOPIAN
      </a>
      (Ketos Aithiopios) A sea-monster killed by Perseus.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="../Ther/KetosTroias.html">
       SEA MONSTER, TROJAN
      </a>
      (Ketos Troios) A sea-monster slain by Heracles.
     </li>
    </ul>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ther/MeloiKhryseoi.html">
      SHEEP, GOLDEN
     </a>
     (Melai Khryseoi) A flock of aggressive and highly poisonous, golden-fleeced sheep.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Pontios/Seirenes.html">
      SIRENS
     </a>
     (Seirenes) Three winged sea-monsters whose irresistable song lured sailors to their deaths. They had the heads (or heads and torsos) of women and the bodies of birds.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ther/Sphinx.html">
      SPHINX
     </a>
     A Theban monster with the head of a woman and the body of a lioness.
    </p>
    <p>
     TURTLE, GIANT A giant turtle preyed on the men cast into the sea by the bandit Sciron.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Thaumasios/HippoiMonokerata.html">
      UNICORNS
     </a>
     Fabulous Indian horses with a single magical horn. The Greeks called them Monocerata (One-Horned) which the Romans translated as Unicorni.
    </p>
