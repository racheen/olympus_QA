     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheKhione1.html">
      CHIONE (1)
     </a>
     (Khione) The goddess  of snow, a daughter of the frosty north-wind Boreas.
    </p>
    <figure class="image-main image-main-50pct">
     <a href="../Ouranios/Kharites.html">
      <img alt="Charites" class="img-responsive center-block" src="../image/F21.1Kharites.jpg"/>

      </a>
     </figcaption>
    </figure>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheKhione2.html">
      CHIONE (2)
     </a>
     (Khione) A snow-nymph loved by the wind-god Boreas.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheKhione3.html">
      CHIONE (3)
     </a>
     (Khione) A Naiad-nymph daughter of the River Nile who was transformed into a snow-cloud by Zeus after she was raped by an Egyptian peasant.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Georgikos/KentaurosKheiron.html">
      CHIRON
     </a>
     (Kheiron) An immortal centaur who was a son of the Titan Cronus and half-brother of the god Zeus. He was a famed teacher who fostered and instructed many great heroes in his cave on Mount Pelion.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheKhloris.html">
      CHLORIS
     </a>
     (Khloris) The goddess of flowers and wife of Zephyrus the west-wind.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Potamos/PotamosKhremetes.html">
      CHREMETES
     </a>
     (Khremetes) A river of Libya and its god.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Protogenos/Khronos.html">
      CHRONOS
     </a>
     (Khronos) The primordial god of time. He and his consort Ananke encircled the cosmos with serpentine coils.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ther/Khrysaor.html">
      CHRYSAOR
     </a>
     (Khrysaor) A gigantic son of the Gorgon Medusa.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ther/KuonKhryseos.html">
      CHRYSEAN DOG
     </a>
     (Khryseos Kuon) A golden hound which was set by Rhea set to guard the infant Zeus and his nurse, the goat Amaltheia.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ther/KriosKhrysomallos.html">
      CHRYSOMALLUS
     </a>
     (Khrysomallos) A flying, golden-fleeced ram sent by the cloud-nymph Nephele to rescue her children, Phrixus and Helle, from a sacrifice to the gods.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheKhrysopeleia.html">
      CHRYSOPELIA
     </a>
     (Khrysopeleia) The nymph wife of Arcas king of Arcadia.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Daimon/Khrysos.html">
      CHRYSOS
     </a>
     (Khrysos) The god of gold.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Georgikos/Khrysothemis.html">
      CHRYSOTHEMIS
     </a>
     (Khrysothemis) The goddess of the "golden custom" of the harvest festival.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Titan/Kirke.html">
      CIRCE
     </a>
     (Kirke) A goddess-witch of the magical island of Aeaea who transformed visitors into beasts with her potions and spells.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Georgikos/OrosKithairon.html">
      CITHAERON
     </a>
     (Kithairon) The ancient, craggy god of Mount Cithaeron in southern Greece.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheKlaia.html">
      CLAEA
     </a>
     (Klaia) An Oread-nymph who possessed a cavern shrine on Mount Calathium in Messenia.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ther/HusKlazomenaios.html">
      CLAZOMENAEAN BOAR
     </a>
     (Hus Klazomenaios) A giant, winged boar which terrorized the Ionian town of Clazomenae.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiHyades.html">
      CLEEIA
     </a>
     (Kleeia) One of the starry Hyad-nymphs.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheKleokhareia.html">
      CLEOCHARIA
     </a>
     (Kleokhareia) The Naiad-nymph wife of Lelex, first king of Sparta.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheKleodora.html">
      CLEODORA
     </a>
     (Kleodora) A nymph of Mount Parnassus loved by the god Poseidon.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheKleomede.html">
      CLEOMEDE
     </a>
     (Kleomede) A nymph of the river Axius loved by King Paeonus of Paeonia.
    </p>
    <figure class="image-main image-main-400">
     <a href="../Ther/KuonKerberos.html">
      <img alt="Cerberus" class="img-responsive center-block" src="../image/M12.1Kerberos.jpg"/>

    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheKleone.html">
      CLEONE
     </a>
     (Kleone) A Naiad-nymph of the town of Cleonae in Argos.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ouranios/MousaKleio.html">
      CLIO
     </a>
     (Kleio) The goddess Muse of history.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheKlonie.html">
      CLONIA
     </a>
     (Klonie) A nymph of the Boeotian town of Hyria.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Daimon/Moirai.html">
      CLOTHO
     </a>
     (Klotho) One of the three Fates.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Titan/TitanisKlymene.html">
      CLYMENE (1)
     </a>
     (Klymene) The Titan-goddess of fame and infamy. She was one of the elder Oceanides and wife of the Titan Iapetus.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheKlymene.html">
      CLYMENE (2)
     </a>
     (Klymene) An Oceanid-nymph who was the mother of Phaethon by the sun-god Helius.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheKlytie.html">
      CLYTIA
     </a>
     (Klytia) An Oceanid nymph loved by the sun-god Helius. When he forsook her, she pined away and was transformed into a sun-gazing heliotrope flower.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Gigante/Gigantes.html">
      CLYTIUS
     </a>
     (Klytios) A Gigante (Giant) who was slain by the flaming torches of Hecate in the war against the gods.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheKnossia.html">
      CNOSSIA
     </a>
     (Knossia) A nymph of the Cretan town of Cnossus who was loved by King Menelaus of Sparta.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Daimon/Koalemos.html">
      COALEMUS
     </a>
     (Koalemos) The personification of stupidity.
    </p>
    <p>
     COCYTHIAE (Kokythiai) Naiad-nymph daughters of the underworld river Cocytus.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Khthonios/PotamosKokytos.html">
      COCYTUS
     </a>
     (Kokytos) The underworld river of wailing and its weeping god.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Titan/TitanKoios.html">
      COEUS
     </a>
     (Koios) The Titan-god of the inquiring mind who presided over the axis of heaven.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheKombe.html">
      COMBE
     </a>
     (Kombe) A rustic nymph of the island of Euboea, mother of the Euboean Corybantes.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Georgikos/SatyrosKomos.html">
      COMUS
     </a>
     (Komos) The god of festivity.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Georgikos/SatyroiOrthanes.html">
      CONISALUS
     </a>
     (Konisalos) A phallic daemon of garden fertility.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheKorkyra.html">
      CORCYRA
     </a>
     (Korkyra) A Naiad-nymph daughter of the river Asopus abducted to the island of Corcyra by the god Poseidon.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiKoronides.html">
      CORONIDES
     </a>
     (Koronides) Twin daughters of the Giant Orion who saved the city of Orchomeus from plague by offering themselves up as a sacrifice to the gods. Persephone, pitying their fate, transformed them into comets.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiHyades.html">
      CORONIS
     </a>
     (Koronis) One of the starry Hyades.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Daimon/Koros.html">
      CORUS
     </a>
     (Koros) The personification of disdain.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Georgikos/Kouretes.html">
      CORYBANTES (1), PHRYGIAN
     </a>
     (Korybantes Phrygioi) Divine attendants of the goddess Cybele who celebrated her rites with a dance of clashing shield and spear.
    </p>
    <figure class="image-main image-main-300">

     </a>
     (Korybantes Samothrakioi) Daemones of the orgiastic dance of the Samothracian mysteries.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Georgikos/KorybantesEuboiai.html">
      CORYBANTES (3), EUBOEAN
     </a>
     (Korybantes Euboiai) Curete-like daemones of the island of Euboea in Greece.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheKorykia.html">
      CORYCIA
     </a>
     (Korykia) A nymph of the Corycian spring of Delphi  loved by the god Apollo.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiKorykiai.html">
      CORYCIAE
     </a>
     (Korykiai) Naiad-nymph daughters of the river Pleistus who tended the springs of the sacred Corycian cave on Mount Parnassus.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Georgikos/Korymbos.html">
      CORYMBUS
     </a>
     (Korymbos) The rustic demigod of  the ivy wreaths worn by celebrants of the Bacchic orgies.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Titan/Hekatonkheires.html">
      COTTUS
     </a>
     (Kottos) One of the hundred-handed, fifty-headed Hecatoncheir-giants.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Thrakios/Kotys.html">
      COTYS
     </a>
     (Kotys) A wild Thracian goddess worshipped with wild Bacchic orgies.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiHamadryades.html">
      CRANEIA
     </a>
     (Kraneia) The Hamadryad-nymph of the cornel or cherry tree.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Pontios/Keto.html">
      CRATAEIS
     </a>
     (Krataeis) The sea-goddess mother of the monster Scylla.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Daimon/Kratos.html">
      CRATUS
     </a>
     (Kratos) The god of strength, a henchman guardian of the throne of Zeus.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/Naiades.html">
      CRENAEAE
     </a>
     (Krenaiai) Naiad-nymphs of fountains.
    </p>
    <p>
     CRES The earth-goddess of the island of Crete.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ther/TaurosKretaios.html">
      CRETAN BULL
     </a>
     (Tauros Kretaios) A fabulous bull sent from the sea by Poseidon in answer to the prayers of Minos of Crete. Queen Pasiphae  fell love with the beast and coupling with it bore the Minotaur. Heracles was later sent to fetch the bull as one of his Twelve Labours.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheKretheis.html">
      CRETHEIS
     </a>
     (Kretheis) A Naiad-nymph of the Lydian town of Smyrna who, according to legend, was the mother of the poet Homer by the river Meles.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheKreousa.html">
      CREUSA
     </a>
     (Kreousa) The Naiad-nymph wife of the river Peneus in northern Greece.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/Naiades.html">
      CRINAEAE
     </a>
     (Krinaiai) Naiad-nymphs of fountains.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Titan/TitanKrios.html">
      CRIUS
     </a>
     (Krios) The Titan-god of mastery and leadership who was imprisoned in Tartarus by the gods.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiArtemisiai.html">
      CROCALE
     </a>
     (Krokale) One of the nymph handmaidens of the goddess Artemis.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ther/HusKrommyon.html">
      CROMMYON SOW
     </a>
     (Hus Krommyon) A giant boar sent by the gods to ravage the farmlands of Crommyon. It was destroyed by the hero Theseus.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Titan/TitanKronos.html">
      CRONUS
     </a>
     (Kronos) The king of the Titans and god of time. He castrated his father Uranus and seized the throne of heaven but was afterwards deposed by his on Zeus. Cronus was cast into Tartarus but later freed and appointed king of Elysium.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <figure class="image-main image-main-400">
     <a href="../Ther/TaurosKretaios.html">
      <img alt="Cretan Bull" class="img-responsive center-block" src="../image/Z26.1ETauros_sm.jpg"/>
     </a>
     <figcaption>
      <a href="../Ther/TaurosKretaios.html">
       CRETAN BULL
      </a>
     </figcaption>
    </figure>
    <p>
     <a href="../Georgikos/SatyrosKrotos.html">
      CROTUS
     </a>
     (Krotos) A satyr companion of the nine Muses who was placed amongst the stars as the constellation Saggitarius.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Daimon/Ktesios.html">
      CTESIUS
     </a>
     (Ktesios) The friendly daemon-guardian of the household.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Georgikos/Kouretes.html">
      CURETES
     </a>
     (Kouretes) Guardians of the infant god Zeus who drowned out his cries with  a dance of clashing spear and shield, so concealing him from Cronus.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Georgikos/EleusiniosKyamites.html">
      CYAMITES
     </a>
     (Kyamites) The bean-man, a minor deity of the Eleusinian Mysteries.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheKyane.html">
      CYANE (1)
     </a>
     (Kyane) The Naiad-nymph of a spring near the town of Syracuse in Sicily. When Hades violated her spring during the abduction of Persephone, she was transformed into water.
    </p>
    <p>
     <span class="heading">
      CYANE (2)
     </span>
     (Kyane) The nymph wife of Aeolus king of the winds.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheKyanee.html">
      CYANEE
     </a>
     (Kyanee) A Naiad-nymph of the springs of the Carian town of Miletus. She was the wife of the town's eponymous founder.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Phrygios/Kybele.html">
      CYBELE
     </a>
     (Kybele) The Phrygian mother of the gods who was identified by the Greeks with the goddess Rhea.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Titan/Kyklopes.html">
      CYCLOPES (1), ELDER
     </a>
     (Kyklopes) Three one-eyed giants imprisoned in Tartarus  by the Titan Cronus. They were rescued by Zeus and in thanks crafted  him his lightning-bolts, an earth-shaking trident for his brother Poseidon and a helm of invisiblity for Hades.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Gigante/GigantesKyklopes.html">
      CYCLOPES (2), YOUNGER
     </a>
     (Kyklopes) A primitive tribe of one-eyed, cannibal giants encountered by Odysseus during his travels.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NymphaiKydnides.html">
      CYDNIDES
     </a>
     (Kydnides) Warlike Cicilian Naiads who fought for Dionysus in his Indian War.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Daimon/Kydoimos.html">
      CYDOIMUS
     </a>
     (Kydoimos) The personification of battlefield confusion and the din of war.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheKyllene.html">
      CYLLENE
     </a>
     (Kyllene) A nymph of Mount Cyllene who was the wife of the Arcadian king Pelasgus.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheKymopoleia.html">
      CYMOPOLEA
     </a>
     (Kymopoleia) The goddess of the waves, wife of the hundred-handed giant Briareus.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Phylos/Kunokephaloi.html">
      CYNOCEPHALI
     </a>
     (Kunokephaloi) A tribe of dog-headed men which dwelt in a remote corner of Africa or India.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Heroine/Kyrene.html">
      CYRENE
     </a>
     (Kyrene) A huntress-nymph of Mount Pelion in Thessaly. The god Apollo happened upon her as she was wrestling a lion and, falling in love, abducted her to Libya where she bore him a son--the demigod Aristaeus.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Potamos/PotamosKytheros.html">
      CYTHERUS
     </a>
     (Kytheros) A river of Elis in southern Greece and its god.
    </p>
