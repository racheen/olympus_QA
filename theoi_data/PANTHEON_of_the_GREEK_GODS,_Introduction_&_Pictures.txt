        <p>
         <span class="heading">
          THE IMMORTALS
         </span>
         of the Ancient Greek pantheon can be divided into roughly eight classes.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="heading">
          THE FIRST
         </span>
         of these were the
         <a href="greek-mythology/primeval-gods.html">
          PROTOGENOI
         </a>
         or First Born gods. These were the primeval beings who emerged at creation to form the  very fabric of universe: Earth, Sea, Sky, Night, Day, etc. Although they were divinites they were purely elemental in form: Gaia was the literal Earth, Pontos the Sea, and Ouranos the Dome of Heaven. However they were sometimes represented assuming anthroporphic shape, albeit ones that were indivisible from their native element. Gaia the earth, for example, might manifest herself as a matronly woman half-risen from the ground ; and Thalassa the sea might lift her head above the waves in the shape of a sea-formed woman.
        </p>

        <p>
         <span class="heading">
          THE SECOND
         </span>
         were the nature
         <span class="heading">
          DAIMONES
         </span>
         (Spirits) and NYMPHAI who nurtured life in the four elements. E.g. fresh-water Naiades, forest Dryades, beast-loving Satyroi, marine Tritones, etc.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="heading">
          THE THIRD
         </span>
         were the body- and mind-affecting
         <a href="greek-mythology/personifications.html">
          DAIMONES
         </a>
         (Spirits). E.g. Sleep (Hypnos), Love (Eros), Joy (Euphrosyne), Hate (Eris), Fear (Phobos), Death (Thanatos), Old Age (Geras), etc.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="heading">
          THE FOURTH
         </span>
         class consisted of  the
         <span class="heading">
          THEOI
         </span>
         (Gods) who controlled  the forces of nature and bestowed civilised arts upon mankind.
        </p>

        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="heading">
          THE FIFTH
         </span>
         were the
         <a href="greek-mythology/olympian-gods.html">
          12 OLYMPIAN GODS
         </a>
         who governed the universe and commanded the legions of lesser gods and spirits. They were, namely, Zeus, Hera, Poseidon, Demeter, Artemis, Apollon, Ares, Athene, Aphrodite, Hephaistos, Hermes, Dionysos, and Hestia.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="heading">
          THE SIXTH
         </span>
         of the immortals were the spirits of the CONSTELLATIONS which circed the heavenly night sky. Every constellation, including the twelve signs of the Zodiac, was possessed of one or more spirits EG Saggitarius was the centaur Kheiron, Gemini the Dioskouroi Twins, etc.
        </p>

        <p>
         <span class="heading">
          THE SEVENTH
         </span>
         class consisted of the fabulous
         <a href="greek-mythology/bestiary.html">
          MONSTERS, BEASTS, GIANTS
         </a>
         of myth. 
                      They were semi-divine creatures, closely related to the gods.E.g.
         <a href="greek-mythology/giants.html">
          GIGANTES
         </a>
         (Giants),
         <a href="greek-mythology/dragons.html">
          DRAKONES
         </a>
         (Dragons), Kentauroi (Centaurs), Kerberos (Cerberus),  Sphinx, Sirens,  etc.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="heading">
          THE EIGHTH
         </span>
         were the
         <span class="heading">
          HEROI HEMITHEOI
         </span>
         (Semi-Divine Heroes) who were worshipped after death as minor divinities. They included great heroes like Akhilleus, Theseus and Perseus; heroines such as Alkmene, Helene and Baubo; and founding kings like Erikhthonios, Kadmos and Pelops.
        </p>
        <p>
         There were many divinities in the Greek pantheon who fell into more than one of these categories. Tykhe (Lady Fortune), for example, can easily be classified under category Two as an Okeanis Nymphe, Three as fortune personified, and Four as a popularly worshipped goddess.
        </p>

        <p class="heading">
         <a name="Olympioi">
         </a>
         (I) THE TWELVE OLYMPIAN GODS
        </p>
        <p>
         The Greek Pantheon was ruled by a council of twelve great gods known as the Olympians, namely  Zeus, Hera, Poseidon, Demeter, Athene, Hephaistos, Ares, Aphrodite, Apollon, Artemis, Hermes, Dionysos, and sometimes Hestia.
         <br/>
         These twelve gods demanded worship from all their subjects. Those who failed to honour any one of the Twelve with due sacrifice and libation were duly punished.
         <br/>
         Directly and through a host of divine minions the Twelve gods governed all aspects of human life.
        </p>
