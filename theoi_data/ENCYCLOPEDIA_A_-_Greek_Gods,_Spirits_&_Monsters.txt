      ARCE
     </a>
     (Arke) A sister of the rainbow-goddess Iris. She was the messenger of the Titan-gods who was stripped of her wings and cast into Tartarus by Zeus.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Olympios/Ares.html">
      ARES
     </a>
     The god of war and bloodshed, one of the twelve supreme Olympians.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Daimon/Arete.html">
      ARETE
     </a>
     The goddesss of virtue and excellence.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheArethousa.html">
      ARETHUSA
     </a>
     (Arethousa) The Naiad-nymph of a Sicilian spring who was chased across the sea  by the lustful river-god Alpheus.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Titan/Kyklopes.html">
      ARGES
     </a>
     One of the three metal-working Cyclops.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheArgiope1.html">
      ARGIOPE (1)
     </a>
     A Nymph of Mount Parnassus who was abandoned by the poet Philammon.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheArgiope2.html">
      ARGIOPE (2)
     </a>
     An Eleusinian nymph.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Gigante/GiganteArgosPanoptes.html">
      ARGUS PANOPTES
     </a>
     (Argos Panoptes) A giant whose body was covered with a hundred eyes. He was set by Hera to guard the maiden Io--a lover of Zeus who the god had disguised as a cow. After he was slain by Hermes, Hera placed his eyes on the tail of the peacock.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheArgyra.html">
      ARGYRA
     </a>
     A sea-nymph of Achaea loved by the hero Selemnus.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Georgikos/Ariadne.html">
      ARIADNE
     </a>
     A Cretan princess who helped  Theseus defeat the Minotaur. During the voyage to Athens she was carried off by the god Dionysus to be his immortal wife.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Phylos/Arimaspoi.html">
      ARIMASPIANS
     </a>
     (Arimaspoi) A tribe of one-eyed Scythians who waged a continual war with the Griffins for mountain gold.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <figure class="image-main image-main-300">
     <a href="../Olympios/Athena.html">
      <img alt="Athena" class="img-responsive center-block" src="../image/K8.3Athena.jpg"/>
     </a>
     <figcaption>
      <a href="../Olympios/Athena.html">
       ATHENA
      </a>
     </figcaption>
    </figure>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ther/HipposAreion.html">
      ARION
     </a>
     (Areion) An immortal horse born of the goddess Demeter after Poseidon had violated her in the guise of a horse. Arion was owned first by Heracles and later by the hero Adrastus.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Georgikos/Aristaios.html">
      ARISTAEUS (1)
     </a>
     (Aristaios) The rustic god of bee-keeping, cheese-making, olive-growing and hunting. He was a companion of his divine cousin Dionysus.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Gigante/GiganteAristaios.html">
      ARISTAEUS (2)
     </a>
     (Aristaios) A Gigante (Giant) who fled the battle with the gods and was transformed into a dung-beetle.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Olympios/Artemis.html">
      ARTEMIS
     </a>
     The goddess of hunting, wild animals, childbirth and children, and one of the twelve great Olympian gods. Her deadly arrows brought sudden death to women and girls.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Georgikos/DaimonesKeramikoi.html">
      ASBETON
     </a>
     The daemon "Charr" which plagued the craftsman-potter.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Khthonios/Askalaphos.html">
      ASCALAPHUS
     </a>
     (Askalaphos) An underworld spirit who tended the orchards of Hades. When he told his master that Persephone had tasted the seeds of the pomegranate Demeter transformed him into an accursed screech-owl.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Potamos/PotamosAskanios.html">
      ASCANIUS
     </a>
     (Askanios) A river of Mysia and its liquid god.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ouranios/Asklepios.html">
      ASCLEPIUS
     </a>
     (Asklepios) The god of medicine. He was a student of the centaur Chiron who became so skilled in the art of healing that he could bring the dead back to life. Zeus struck him dead with a lightning-bolt for defying the natural order.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheAskre.html">
      ASCRA
     </a>
     (Askre) A Boeotian nymph loved by the god Poseidon.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Titan/TitanisKlymene.html">
      ASIA (1)
     </a>
     (Asie) The Oceanid wife of the Titan Iapetus who gave her name to a continent.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheAsie.html">
      ASIA (2)
     </a>
     (Asie) The Oceanid wife of the Titan Prometheus who gave her name to the continent of Asia.
    </p>
    <p>
     ASOPIS A Naiad daughter of the river Asopus in southern Greece.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Potamos/PotamosAsopos.html">
      ASOPUS
     </a>
     (Asopos) A river of Boeotia and Argos in southern Greece and its watery-god. He had twenty beautiful daughters who were all abducted by the gods. When he tried to recover his daughter Aegina from Zeus, the god scorched him with a lightning-bolt.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Titan/TitanisAsteria.html">
      ASTERIA
     </a>
     The Titan-goddess of nocturnal  rites and mother of the witch Hecate. When Zeus pursued her, she transformed herself into a quail and leaping from heaven into the sea became the island of Delos.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Potamos/PotamosAsterion.html">
      ASTERION
     </a>
     A river of Argos in the southern Greece and its god.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Gigante/GiganteAnaxAsterios.html">
      ASTERIUS
     </a>
     (Asterios) A Lydian giant.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheAsterodeia.html">
      ASTERODEIA
     </a>
     A nymph of the Caucasus mountains loved by King Aeetes of Colchis.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheAsterope2.html">
      ASTEROPE
     </a>
     An Oceanid-nymph loved by Zeus.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Titan/AstraPlaneta.html">
      ASTRA PLANETA
     </a>
     The gods of the five wandering stars or planets.  Hesperus was the spirit of the star Venus and Pyroeis of Mars.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Titan/Astraia.html">
      ASTRAEA
     </a>
     (Astraia) The Titan-goddess of justice. She left the earth at the end of the Golden Age to become the constellation Virgo.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <figure class="image-main image-main-300">
     <a href="../Titan/TitanAtlas.html">
      <img alt="Atlas" class="img-responsive center-block" src="../image/T20.1Atlas.jpg"/>
     </a>
     <figcaption>
      <a href="../Titan/TitanAtlas.html">
       ATLAS
      </a>
     </figcaption>
    </figure>
    <p>
     <a href="../Titan/TitanAstraios.html">
      ASTRAEUS (1)
     </a>
     (Astraios) The Titan-god of the stars and the arts of astronomy and astrology. He was the father of  the four winds and the wandering stars (planets) by the dawn-goddess Eos.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Georgikos/SeilenosAstraios.html">
      ASTRAEUS (2)
     </a>
     (Astraios) One of the Sileni--elderly satyrs in the retinue of Dionysus.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Titan/Astrape.html">
      ASTRAPE
     </a>
     The goddess of lightning, a handmaiden of the storm-god Zeus.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheAstris.html">
      ASTRIS
     </a>
     A nymph daughter of Helius the sun and wife of the Indian river-god Hydaspes.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="star-myths.html">
      ASTROTHESIAI
     </a>
     The spirits of constellations. They mostly mortal men and creatures granted a place of honour amongst the stars of heaven
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheAstyokhe.html">
      ASTYOCHE
     </a>
     (Astyokhe) A Naiad-nymph daughter of the Trojan river Simoeis who married a king of Troy.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Daimon/Ate.html">
      ATE
     </a>
     The personification of error, delusion and blind folly who led  men and gods alike to rash and inconsiderate action. Zeus grabbed her by the hair and tossed her out of heaven.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Olympios/Athena.html">
      ATHENA
     </a>
     (Athene) The goddess of wisdom, war and  crafts, and one of the twelve supreme Olympians. She sprang fully grown and ready-armed from the head of Zeus.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/NympheAtlanteiaPhoibe.html">
      ATLANTEIA
     </a>
     A Dryad-nymph loved by King Danaus of Libya.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Phylos/Atlantes.html">
      ATLANTIS
     </a>
     (Atlantis) A lost island which was sunk beneath the sea by the gods as punishment for its people's wanton immorality.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Titan/TitanAtlas.html">
      ATLAS
     </a>
     The Titan-god of daring who led the Titans in their war against the gods. He was condemned to bear the solid-dome of heaven upon his shoulders.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Daimon/Moirai.html">
      ATROPUS
     </a>
     (Atropos) One of the three Fates.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Phrygios/Attis.html">
      ATTIS
     </a>
     A eunuch attendant of the Phrygian goddess Cybele and the charioteer of her lion-drawn car. The goddess caused him to castrate himself when she discovered him in the arms of a nymph.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Titan/Horai.html">
      AUGE
     </a>
     One of the Horae of the hours. She presided over the dawn-hour of first light.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/Dryades.html">
      AULONIADES
     </a>
     Dryad-nymphs of the glens.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Titan/Aura.html">
      AURA
     </a>
     The Titan-goddess of the breeze who was violated by Dionysus in the mountains of Phrygia.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Nymphe/Aurai.html">
      AURAE
     </a>
     (Aurai) Nymphs of the breezes.
     <img alt="Illustrated" src="../image/eye.gif"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ther/Automotones.html">
      AUTOMATONES
     </a>
     Animated metal statues of animals and men crafted by the divine smith Hephaestus.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Ouranios/HoraAuxesia.html">
      AUXESIA
     </a>
     The Hora of spring growth.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="../Potamos/PotamosAxios.html">
      AXIUS
     </a>
     (Axios) A river of Paeonia and its watery god.
    </p>
